History

30 years of celebrating the visual arts in Portland

Celebrating 30 years of art in Portland

In 1986, an ambitious 24-year-old gallery owner on SW Pine Street had a vision to make Portland a hub for the visual arts. She gathered a group of fellow gallery owners and hatched a plan: keep their galleries open late one night per month for after-hours public viewings, refreshments, and conversations. They would call it "First Thursday" and all would be welcome — first-time visitors could browse alongside more seasoned art collectors. Thirty years later we are continuing to keep that spirit alive.
